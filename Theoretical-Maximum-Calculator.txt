PART 1 — PHYSICS CONSTANTS (from EnergyCalculator.cs)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Energy constant K         = 2.0
  Move cost formula         = K × speed²  (per tick)
  Day solar charge          = +10% per tick
  Mining drain              = -2% per tick
  Standby drain             = -1% per tick

  Speed values (cells per tick):
    Slow   = 1  →  move cost = 2×1²  =  2%/tick
    Normal = 2  →  move cost = 2×2²  =  8%/tick
    Fast   = 3  →  move cost = 2×3²  = 18%/tick

  Net battery delta per tick:

    Action        Speed   Daytime          Nighttime
    ──────────    ─────   ──────────────   ──────────────
    Move          Slow    +10 - 2  = +8%   0 - 2  =  -2%
    Move          Normal  +10 - 8  = +2%   0 - 8  =  -8%
    Move          Fast    +10 - 18 = -8%   0 - 18 = -18%
    Mine          —       +10 - 2  = +8%   0 - 2  =  -2%
    Standby       —       +10 - 1  = +9%   0 - 1  =  -1%

  Sol structure:
    Ticks  0–31  = DAY   (32 ticks = 16 hours)
    Ticks 32–47  = NIGHT (16 ticks =  8 hours)


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PART 2 — THE HARD FLOOR: MINIMUM TICKS PER MINERAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Collecting any mineral unconditionally requires exactly two engine actions:

    1. MOVE action — to reach the mineral cell          (≥ 1 tick)
    2. MINE action — to collect it once standing on it  (= 1 tick, always)

  Therefore: minimum cost per mineral = 1 (move) + 1 (mine) = 2 ticks.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PART 3 — THE RETURN CONSTRAINT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The mission requires the rover to return to base (Start tile) before
  tick 48. A mission that ends with the rover elsewhere is a failure,
  all collected minerals are forfeit.

  In the ideal adjacent-cluster scenario the rover is always 1 step from
  base, so the return costs exactly 1 tick.

  Budget equation:
    Total ticks                           = 48
    Ticks reserved for final return home  =  1
    Ticks available for collect cycles    = 47
    Minerals = floor(47 / 2)             = 23

  ── Why the physics ceiling of 23 is unreachable on this map ───────────────

  The physics ceiling assumes every mineral is 1 step from the rover at all
  times. On the real map the nearest mineral is 1 step away, but only 5
  minerals exist within 3 steps of start. After those are exhausted the rover
  must travel 7+ steps to the next cluster, consuming multiple ticks per
  mineral instead of 1. The 2-tick/mineral ideal breaks at mineral #6.

  Wait — 9 ticks at -2% each = -18%. Battery: 70% - 18% = 52%.
  But we only have 4 ticks left (44→48). Slow covers 1 step/tick = 9 ticks.
  The actual return path is 9 steps at Slow = 9 ticks. That overshoots.

  Re-check: distance from (41,35) to start (34,32) via A* = 9 steps.
  Ticks remaining after #21: 48 - 44 = 4 ticks. 4 < 9. Won't make it at Slow.

    3 ticks × -18%/tick = -54%. Battery: 70% - 54% = 16%.

  ── Exhaustive proof that no 22nd mineral is reachable ─────────────────────

  After mineral #21: position (41,35), tick 44, battery 70%, ticks left = 4.

  Minimum ticks for any 22nd mineral = t_to + 1 (mine) + t_home.
  Even the nearest possible candidate is 1 step away with 9 steps home:
    t_to=1 + mine=1 + t_home(Fast, 9 steps)=3 = 5 ticks needed.
    Ticks available = 4.

  TIME runs out before battery does. There is simply no mineral reachable
  and mineable with a valid return in the remaining 4 ticks from this position.

  Could an alternative 21-mineral route leave the rover closer to home?
  No — the entire mineral chain from #16 onward runs through the (39–44, 34–35)
  cluster, which is 7–10 steps from start. There is no 21-mineral route that
  ends within 4 Fast-ticks of home (i.e. within 12 steps), because all
  remaining unvisited minerals after #15 are in that same region.
